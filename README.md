Framework 1.1: Practical Applications — Aging and Cancer
Author: Yuri Dmitrochenko, MD — Independent Researcher, Israel
Status: Working draft, 2026
Predecessor: Framework 1.0: Hydrogel Biophysics, Tissue Energetics, and Nonlinear Dynamics
About
Framework 1.1 is a ten-article monograph applying the theoretical apparatus introduced in Framework 1.0 — the hydrogel model of the cell, the Basal Glycolytic Regime (BGR), and the Regeneration Pendulum — to two concrete biomedical domains: oncogenesis and aging.
If Framework 1.0 laid out the language, Framework 1.1 is the first attempt to speak it: to test whether the same conceptual apparatus can, without translation, address specific clinical phenomena such as neuroblastoma 4S, the bifurcation point of energetic reserve, or endometrial hyperplasia.
All articles are theoretical/conceptual work products of a human–AI research tandem: conceptual architecture originates with the author, with AI assistance (Claude, Anthropic) used for literature search, formalization, critical review, and manuscript preparation. Predictions throughout are explicitly marked as requiring empirical validation.
Structure
The monograph is organized into three parts.
Part I — The Nuclear Glycogen Trilogy
Three articles on nuclear glycogen as a metabolic and structural compartment: local glycolysis and nuclear syneresis, nuclear glycogen as a determinant of tumor resistance, and glycogen as a graded marker of cellular differentiation across developmental and evolutionary contexts.
Part II — The Clone and the Organism
Five articles developing a unified dynamic model of oncogenesis built around two bifurcation points and a twelve-variable state space:
	1.	Pathological Clone Formation — the differentiation information pyramid and the regenerative energy window
	2.	The Onset of Clone–Organism Interaction — the first bifurcation, the autoimmune zone, and the immune response paradox
	3.	Capture of the Tissue Oscillator — the second bifurcation, ECM degradation, and malignant progression
	4.	Towards Optimisation of Cancer Prevention and Treatment — level-specific suppressor therapy (LSST), grounded in the spontaneous regression of neuroblastoma Stage 4S
	5.	Energy-Temporal Reserve and Clinical Trajectory — an actuarial approach to oncological decision-making in older patients, using energy reserve R(t) and the bifurcation point T* (dedicated to Sarah-Lana Demi, Data Scientist)
Supplementary working notes extending this series — horizontal (quorum-sensing) suppression as a second control circuit, and a closing chapter on three cellular states and the energetic limit of clonal escape, illustrated by endometrial hyperplasia — are included as additional material.
Part III — The Energetics of Aging
Two articles modeling age-related decline in integral energetic indicators (mitochondrial activity, oxygen capacity, basal metabolism, energy reserve), introducing the Structural Maintenance Load (SML) hypothesis and an age-dependent energetic corridor, with the bifurcation point T* (≈76 years) marking the threshold where energy reserve falls below a sustainable margin.
Methodology
Each article follows a consistent epistemic standard: predictions are explicitly flagged as model output requiring verification, and a dedicated section addresses potential sources of model failure and the boundaries of applicability. The work is offered as a framework for discussion, not as established clinical guidance.
Status of this repository
Individual articles have been assembled and cross-checked for terminological and logical consistency (shared variable numbering, consistent bifurcation-point definitions, and explicit cross-references between articles). Final assembly into a single monograph — with cover, preface, table of contents, and afterword — is in progress.
